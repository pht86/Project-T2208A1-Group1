How much aerobic exercise do you need?

The American Heart AssociationTrusted Source recommends 30 minutes or more of aerobic exercise five or more days each week. That can be broken up, though. For example, you can take three 10-minute walks throughout the day.

You should also add in two or more anaerobic strengthening sessions each week that focus on major muscle groups.

Exercise safety

If you’re new to exercise, see your doctor. They can assess your health and recommend a fitness routine that’s safe and effective for you.

Always start with a warm-up and end with a cool-down and stretch. Focus on form, and stop if it hurts.

The Bottom Line

You should begin to see improvements in your cardiovascular endurance by consistently practicing these exercises. Make sure your exercise plan includes both aerobic and anaerobic activities.

Get plenty of rest, stay hydrated, and try to practice these exercises for at least 150 minutes each week.

Talk with your doctor before starting any new exercise program. This is especially important if you have any medical conditions or take any medications.
